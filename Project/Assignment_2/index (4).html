Historic Attractions

Explore Florence's most iconic landmarks and architectural marvels

Ponte Vecchio

Ponte Vecchio

The iconic medieval bridge spanning the Arno River, famous for its jewelry shops and stunning sunset views.

Piazzale Michelangelo

Piazzale Michelangelo

The most famous viewpoint in Florence, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the entire city and the Arno valley.

Boboli Gardens

Boboli Gardens

Historic park behind Pitti Palace, featuring Renaissance landscaping, sculptures, and peaceful walking paths.

Piazza della Signoria

Piazza della Signoria

The political heart of Florence, home to Palazzo Vecchio and an open-air sculpture gallery with masterpieces.

San Miniato al Monte

San Miniato al Monte

Beautiful Romanesque basilica perched on a hilltop, offering spectacular views and exquisite marble inlay work.

Palazzo Pitti

Palazzo Pitti

Renaissance palace housing multiple museums and galleries, including the Palatine Gallery with works by Raphael and Titian.

Things to Do

Immerse yourself in Florence's rich culture and vibrant activities

Visit Uffizi Gallery

Explore one of the world's most famous art museums, home to masterpieces by Botticelli, Michelangelo, and Leonardo da Vinci.

Shop in Oltrarno

Discover authentic artisan workshops and boutiques in the charming Oltrarno district, perfect for unique souvenirs and crafts.

Take Cooking Classes

Learn to prepare authentic Tuscan dishes with local chefs, from handmade pasta to traditional desserts and wine pairings.

Relax in Cascine Park

Enjoy Florence's largest public park, perfect for jogging, picnicking, or simply relaxing along the Arno River.

Experience Nightlife

Enjoy aperitivo culture, rooftop bars with stunning views, and vibrant nightlife in the historic center and trendy neighborhoods.

Wine Tasting Tours

Explore the renowned Chianti wine region with guided tastings at historic vineyards and cellars, learning about Tuscan winemaking traditions.
